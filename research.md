## 5. Tips

### Activity Tips (March-specific)

1. **Layer up and waterproof:** March weather is unpredictable (5-10C, frequent drizzle). Bring a waterproof jacket and dress in layers. Mornings are cold but afternoons can feel surprisingly mild in the sun.

2. **Have indoor backup plans:** For every outdoor activity, have an indoor alternative ready. If it rains during your planned Jordaan walk, duck into Foodhallen or catch a film at Roze Filmdagen instead. The canal cruise is also ideal for rainy weather (heated boats with blankets).

3. **Rent bikes for dry spells:** Amsterdam is best explored by bike even in March. Rental is cheap (~10 EUR/day) and the city is completely flat. Use dry windows for cycling Vondelpark or riding to the cherry blossoms. March means fewer tourists so cycling is more pleasant than summer.

### Restaurant Tips

1. **Book ahead for Bonboon and Morris & Bella:** These fine dining spots require advance reservation. Bonboon is advance-booking only. Morris & Bella is small and intimate. Book as soon as possible for your preferred evening.

2. **Check days carefully:** Several restaurants are closed on specific days. Madre is closed Sunday, Koffie ende Koeck is closed Sunday and Monday, Men Impossible is closed Tuesday. Plan your restaurant schedule around these closures. Your best dinner options for Sunday (March 22) are Bonboon, H/eart.h, Meatless District, or Vegan Junk Food Bar.

3. **Madre for the best father-daughter evening:** The cocktail bar, lively atmosphere, and late hours (open until 01:00) make Madre the ideal choice for a fun evening out together. Book for Thursday or Friday evening and start with cocktails at the bar before dinner.
